Sindhutai Sapkal

About

Sindhutai Sapkal was an Indian social worker
and social activist known particularly for her
work in raising orphaned children in India.

Born: 14 November 1948, Wardha

Died: 4 January 2022, Pune

Parents: Abhiman Sathe

Awards: Padma Shri

Honours: Mother Teresa Awards (2013), Nari Shakti Puraskar (2017), Padma Shri (2021).

Sindhutai Sapkal affectionally called Mai by her many children was unlike anybody else. She took care of thousands of orphans, fought for the rehabilitation of 84 Adivasi villages, and adopted all the orphan children from affected Adivasi tribes to give them a better life.

She knew she wanted to help others life her. Her own painful journey was a lesson to her, which made her empathise with other unfortunate children. She devoted her entire life to her orphans. She loves, nurtures, and protects her children despite the fact that she has no steady means of income.
